01 / What I do

Three domains, one seam: the place where product, compliance, and operations meet.

Architecture is the most expensive point of failure. These are the three disciplines I spend my practice at.

01 Beachhead

Identity architecture

I design KYC, KYB, fraud, and document verification architectures that survive real regulatory review and scale across jurisdictions.

  • Scope regulatory requirements down to a minimum viable architecture.
  • Select the right mix of verification data, risk signals, and manual review.
  • Advise on vendor selection and jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction rollout.
  • Translate compliance requirements into product decisions, and back.
02 Lever

Process automation

I turn manual operations work that bleeds teams dry into compounding systems: documented, measurable, and owned.

  • Spot the repeated human work inside onboarding and implementation.
  • Design automation that removes toil without hiding the decision.
  • Build runbooks so knowledge survives every handoff.
  • Treat internal tooling as a product with its own roadmap.
03 Bet

Applied AI

I'm betting seriously on AI agent fleets as the next layer of operational efficiency, and I'm building in the open to find out what actually works.

  • Prototype AI agent systems for the workflows I know intimately.
  • Ship working examples. Write about what breaks and why.
  • Frame it as compounding human judgment across the operational surface.
  • Treat it as craft. Ship working examples, write about what breaks.

04 / About

The longer story: how I got here and why it compounds.

I'm a solutions architect working at the intersection of enterprise automation, identity verification, and applied AI. Seven years across pre-sales, post-sales, and implementation. First building enterprise integration and automation at Workato, now designing KYC, KYB, fraud, and document verification architectures at Trulioo for cross-border fintechs, marketplaces, and platforms operating across SEA, EU, UK, US, and AU.

My current focus is building and publishing on AI agent fleets for operational workflows (compliance, onboarding, implementation), framed around compounding human judgment across the decisions that matter. I take the builder-in-the-arena view: fewer arguments about what agents will eventually do, more shipping of what they do now.

Earlier, I co-founded and ran operations for GamerDraft, an esports engagement platform that scaled to 5,000+ users in its first six months. Being the one person who owned both the product decision and the customer call (and then the billing error at 11pm) permanently changed how I think about architecture.

Bilingual English and Mandarin; comfortable across the stakeholders, languages, and jurisdictions that cross-border identity work actually requires.
