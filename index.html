What I Believe

Operating principles
i.

Everything is a system. Learn to read one and you can read almost any of them.

ii.

Complexity isn't the enemy — confusion is. The work is making the tangled thing legible.

iii.

The fastest way to understand anything is to find what it's connected to.

iv.

Range beats depth when the world keeps changing the question.

v.

Stay a student. The moment I think I've got it figured out, I've stopped learning.

vi.

Build the thing. Opinions are cheap; working artifacts aren't.
